Need help planning your trip to Spain?


If you are searching for a quick and easy-to-read guide about structuring an independent journey to Spain, this is your book.

This unique paperback is written as a friendly conversation between Author and Reader about planning and preparing for a vacation to Spain.

Spain for the Independent Traveler (SFTIT) is a carefully curated and compiled resource detailing the research conducted for a recent trip to Madrid, Seville, Marbella, Malaga, and Granada. It details an organized process on how to customize and personalize a thoughtful itinerary - to meet the specific needs of the independent traveler.

SFTIT is written as a complement (not as a competitor) to the encyclopedic-style travel books. In fact, this book will help readers use traditional guidebooks - which lists a multitude of hotel options and tourist attractions - more effectively.

This niche book is designed to help travelers process the overwhelming choices involved in booking hotels, finding locally sourced tour operators, purchasing train tickets, discovering flight tips, and deciding what types of insurance may be useful on your future trip to Spain.



COMING SOON – MARCH 2024 – Writing Your College Admission Essay is as easy as cooking GUMBO!

Final edit stage. This book is a fun way to address an extremely serious topic – the college admission essay. Most high school seniors feel the enormous pressure of producing a ‘publish-worthy’ essay. Sometimes the college-bound student needs a lighter lens to view the task at hand. Because this book is written in a recipe format, it is both entertaining and easy-to-follow. Why did I write this book? It was August 2019. A stressed parent hired me as a college consultant to help their struggling senior. She was having trouble writing even an early rough draft of her application essay. So, just for her I typed out a manuscript using my favorite Louisiana Gumbo recipe as a format. Even though the student had never tasted gumbo (much less cooked the dish in real life), it was the key that unlocked the writer’s block.
